Willkommenstext:

Dear participant,

Many thanks for supporting and participating in this study.

The human-machine interface plays a big role in terms of (semi-)automated driving, which will be possible in the future. Current research and implementation of increasingly advanced systems for automated vehicles creates additional challenges. These challenges occur above all when developing the communication between the system and driver in the form of the human-machine interface (HMI). The HMI is intended to provide the driver with important information so that they are aware of the automation status at all times, for instance, and in order to facilitate transitions between the automation levels.

Various HMI versions have been developed for this study to research the usability of these HMIs in the interaction and communication between the system and driver. In this study you will drive various routes with one of these HMIs. The HMI will communicate to you the availability of different automation levels, as well as driver- and system-initiated transitions between the automation levels in the combined display, which should be recognized or carried out by you. The automation levels in this study extend to automated driving.

You will receive a more detailed explanation before the start of the test drive.

Please note that there are no right or wrong answers. The answers will also be collected pseudonymously.

Thank you very much.

Dear participant,

We hereby ask you to participate in the study: “User study in an instrumented vehicle for usability assessment of human-machine interfaces for automated driving”.

 

Responsibility of the study

The study is being carried out by Ms. Deike Albers, M.Sc. at the Department for Ergonomics at the Technical University of Munich. The study is taking place as part of the BMW project Multilab.

 

Purpose

The introduction of vehicles with systems for semi-automated driving (SAE Level 3) is expected in the coming years. The human-machine interface (HMI) plays a central role in the development. When developing the HMIs, special emphasis must be placed on the usability for the user. Thanks to the real vehicle study, the usability of the system and the behavior of potential users can be analyzed in a very real, but at the same time standardized environment.

 

Research question

As part of this research work, the usability of human-machine interfaces for automated driving is to be evaluated. To do this, various methods for collecting objective data (e.g. eye tracking) and subjective data (e.g. questionnaires) are used.

 

What is involved

During the user study, you will be asked to drive various test routes following an introduction and training phase. There will be a short survey between each of the routes. The overall test will last approximately 120 minutes, including filling out the questionnaires.

 

Risks

A real vehicle study in principle entails the same risks as driving a vehicle. However, extensive measures have been taken to minimize the risks for test participants. For example, you will not have to drive faster than 30 km/h. Please always follow the instructions of the test leader when driving. They are available at all times for any questions or doubts you may have.

 

Voluntary participation in the test

Participation in this study is voluntary. You can end your participation in the test at any time without having to give reasons. Refusal to participate or withdrawing from the study early will not have any negative consequences for you.

 

Data protection

In this study, the Department for Ergonomics is responsible for data processing. Any data identifying you personally shall only be used as part of the Multilab project. This includes your age or gender, for example. All data directly identifying you personally will be replaced by an identification code (pseudonymized). This largely excludes the possibility of you being personally identified by unauthorized persons. A collected, anonymized data set without personally identifying data shall be passed on to the industry partner and sponsor of the BMW AG study for research and development purposes.

Sound, image and video material is also recorded during the test using installed microphones, cameras and eye tracking. This material is used for later evaluation. There is no recording of the face. In the event that extracts of the image material are used in public contributions (e.g. presentations at conferences or journal contributions), it shall be ensured that it is not possible to identify you personally.

Your data will be stored by the Department for Ergonomics in the archive of the Leibniz Supercomputing Centre and by BMW AG. The industry partner and sponsor of the BMW AG study is also subject to the European General Data Protection Regulation. The data will be deleted after the statutory deletion periods have expired. Consent to processing your data is voluntary. You can revoke your consent at any time without having to give reasons and without any negative consequences. You have the right to obtain information about the data concerned, including a copy free of charge. You can also request that your data be corrected or deleted.
